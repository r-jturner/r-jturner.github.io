Research

Peculiar Velocities

All galaxies have velocities that are some combination of their movement in an expanding universe and their peculiar motion. Galaxy peculiar velocities are caused by the gravitational influence of large-scale structure, and thus can be used as a probe of the underlying distribution of matter in the Universe. I use peculiar velocities to constrain the growth rate of structure in the Universe, which can be used as a direct test of General Relativity and modified theories of gravity.

Velocity Field Reconstructions

The galaxy density field is related to the peculiar velocity field in linear theory, and so we can use the observed galaxy distribution in a volume to infer the velocity field at any point in that volume. I use a modified version of techniques used to reconstruct the Baryon Acoustic peak in order to make accurate reconstructions of the velocity field that can be used to improve redshift measurements for local distance indicators such as Type Ia supernovae and gravitational wave events.

About Me

I am a Postdoctoral Research Associate at Swinburne University of Technology in Melbourne, Australia. I am interested in all things cosmology, but the focus of my research is in peculiar velocity cosmology. I use statistical methods to measure the growth rate of large-scale structure and expansion history of the Universe to test cosmological models and theories of gravity, better understand dark energy, and resolve the Hubble tension. I am also interested in large spectrographic surveys, and I am a member of the Dark Energy Spectroscopic Instrument (DESI) and the 4MOST Hemisphere Survey (4HS) collaborations. My general scientific interests are:

  • Peculiar velocities
  • Statistics
  • Large-scale structure
  • Dark energy
  • Spectroscopic surveys
  • Weak lensing
  • Baryon acoustic oscillations
Beyond my research, I am passionate about science communication and outreach. I am an experienced public speaker, educator, and science communicator. I have given many guided 3D tours of the Solar System for students of various age ranges and experiences at Swinburne Univesity, led work experience activities for high-school students, and have given public talks on cosmology. I have also written for The Conversation, engaged with the media for DESI press releases, and contributed a chapter for a to-be-published Encyclopedia of Astrophysics.
